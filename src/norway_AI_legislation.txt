KINGDOM OF NORWAY
PROPOSED LEGISLATION

File Name: norway_AI_legislation.txt
Bill ID: NAISRA-2026-01
Title: National Artificial Intelligence Security and Research Advancement Act
Date: 22 February 2026

PREAMBLE

Recognizing the strategic importance of artificial intelligence to national security, economic resilience, and technological sovereignty;
Acknowledging the role of advanced research and development in maintaining Norway’s competitiveness and safeguarding critical infrastructure;
The Storting enacts the following:

SECTION 1 — PURPOSE

1.1 This Act establishes a national framework to:
    a) Strengthen Norway’s national security posture through secure AI development and deployment.
    b) Accelerate domestic AI research and development (R&D).
    c) Protect critical infrastructure and defense-relevant technologies.
    d) Promote sovereign technological capabilities within Norwegian territory.

SECTION 2 — DEFINITIONS

2.1 “Strategic AI System” means any artificial intelligence system deployed within:
    a) Defense or military operations.
    b) Energy, maritime, telecommunications, or financial infrastructure.
    c) Government decision-support systems impacting national security.
2.2 “Critical Infrastructure Operator” means any public or private entity designated by the Government as essential to national stability.
2.3 “National AI Research Institution” means any accredited Norwegian university, research institute, or authorized laboratory conducting AI R&D under this Act.

SECTION 3 — NATIONAL AI SECURITY STANDARDS

3.1 All Strategic AI Systems shall comply with national cybersecurity standards established by the Ministry of Justice and Public Security.
3.2 Providers of Strategic AI Systems must:
    a) Maintain secure model training environments within approved jurisdictions.
    b) Implement access controls and continuous monitoring.
    c) Conduct adversarial resilience testing prior to deployment.
3.3 Foreign acquisition of Strategic AI Systems or critical AI assets shall be subject to national security review.

SECTION 4 — NATIONAL AI RESEARCH FUND

4.1 A National AI Research and Innovation Fund is hereby established.
4.2 The Fund shall allocate grants to:
    a) Advanced machine learning research.
    b) Maritime autonomy and oceanic sensing technologies.
    c) AI-enabled energy optimization systems.
    d) Secure edge computing and sovereign compute infrastructure.
4.3 Priority shall be given to collaborative projects between industry, universities, and government agencies.

SECTION 5 — DEFENSE AND DUAL-USE TECHNOLOGY DEVELOPMENT

5.1 The Ministry of Defence shall establish a Strategic AI Acceleration Program focused on:
    a) Autonomous maritime systems.
    b) Secure command-and-control decision-support models.
    c) Cyber defense automation.
5.2 Dual-use technologies developed under this Act shall be governed by export control regulations to protect national interests.

SECTION 6 — TALENT AND EDUCATION INITIATIVES

6.1 The Government shall expand AI-focused academic programs at Norwegian universities.
6.2 Scholarships and research fellowships shall be awarded to Norwegian citizens pursuing advanced AI research in security-relevant domains.
6.3 Public-private partnerships shall support workforce retraining in AI-related sectors.

SECTION 7 — DATA SOVEREIGNTY AND COMPUTE INFRASTRUCTURE

7.1 Sensitive national datasets used in Strategic AI Systems must be stored and processed within Norway or approved allied jurisdictions.
7.2 The Government shall invest in high-performance computing infrastructure to support domestic AI research.
7.3 Critical AI models used in national security contexts shall not rely exclusively on foreign-controlled cloud infrastructure without review.

SECTION 8 — OVERSIGHT AND GOVERNANCE

8.1 A National AI Security Council is hereby established to:
    a) Oversee implementation of this Act.
    b) Issue annual assessments of AI-related security risks.
    c) Recommend updates to national AI strategy.
8.2 The Council shall report annually to the Storting.

SECTION 9 — ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES

9.1 Violations involving unauthorized transfer of Strategic AI technologies may result in:
    a) Administrative fines.
    b) Revocation of operating licenses.
    c) Criminal penalties as defined under national security law.
9.2 Grant recipients found non-compliant with security requirements shall be subject to funding suspension or repayment obligations.

SECTION 10 — EFFECTIVE DATE

10.1 This Act shall enter into force 180 days following adoption by the Storting.

SIGNATURE BLOCK

Adopted by the Storting
Kingdom of Norway
